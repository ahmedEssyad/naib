## Definition

A **sole proprietorship** (entreprise individuelle) is a simplified business form, suited to **commercial, industrial, or artisanal** activities. This status:

- requires **no minimum capital**;
- does **not allow a partner** — the entrepreneur is alone;
- creates **no separate legal personality**: the business's assets are merged with the owner's. The entrepreneur is **personally liable** for all the business's obligations.

It's the simplest and fastest form to set up.

## Full file required

1. **Unified Business Creation Form**, duly filled in and signed.
2. **Four (4) photocopies of the applicant's national ID card**, or **residence card** for resident foreigners, or **passport** for non-resident foreigners.
3. **A marriage certificate extract** (or any official document equivalent to it, mentioning the matrimonial regime) — **only if the applicant is a foreigner**.
4. **Two (2) certified copies of the property title or lease agreement** for the premises + **one electricity or water bill**.
5. **A photocopy of the administrative authorisation to carry out the declared activity**, if the activity requires one (health, education, transport, etc.).
6. **Two (2) photocopies of the bank account opening certificate**.
7. **Two (2) photocopies of the ID of each declared employee** (if the business already employs staff at creation).
8. **Payment of the Commercial Court registry fees**, via the mobile-banking accounts opened for this purpose (200 MRU for sole proprietorships).

## Legal basis

- **Decree no. 2022-169 of 17 November 2022**, repealing and replacing Decree no. 2014-076 of 29 May 2014, on the creation of the unified business-creation application form.
- **Order no. 1487/MJ/2021 of 13 December 2021**, defining the forms for applications to register in the local Commercial Registry, beneficial-ownership declarations, and the forms for registration/searches in the Movable Securities Register.
- **Decree no. 2021-033 of 11 March 2021**, on the Commercial Registry and Movable Securities Register.
- **Law no. 2021-005 of 10 February 2021**, amending and supplementing Law no. 2000-005 of 18 January 2000 (Commercial Code).
- **Law no. 2019-018 of 29 April 2019** establishing the General Tax Code (in force since 1 January 2020).
- **Law no. 2021-007 of 22 February 2021**, amending Law no. 67-039 of 3 February 1967 establishing the Social Security regime.
- **Order no. 1974-116 of 17 September 1974**, on terms for employer affiliation and contribution payments to the CNSS.
