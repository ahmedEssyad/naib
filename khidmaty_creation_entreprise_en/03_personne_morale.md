## Definition

A **personne morale** (corporate entity) is an entity endowed with **separate legal personality**: it directly holds rights and obligations, distinct from the natural or legal persons that make it up or that created it.

Mauritanian law recognises several forms:

- **SARL** (Limited Liability Company) — the most common form for SMEs.
- **SA** (Public Limited Company) — for businesses with larger capital.
- **Cooperatives** and other specific entities (professional, mutualist, etc.).

Key advantage: partners' liability is **limited to their contributions**.

## Full file required

1. **Unified Business Creation Form**, duly filled in and signed.
2. **One certified copy and three (3) photocopies of the Articles of Association** + **one electricity or water bill** for the registered office.
3. **A certificate of deposit of the Articles of Association at the Commercial Court registry**.
4. **Four (4) photocopies of the ID of the manager/director and of the partners**.
5. **Two (2) photocopies of the ID of each declared employee**.
6. **A certificate of deposit of the portion of capital to be released**, issued by the depositary — **or** a declaration by the manager that the amount has been collected, **when the share capital exceeds 10,000 MRU**.
7. **Two (2) photocopies of the bank account opening certificate in the company's name**.
8. **A photocopy of the authorisation needed to carry out the activity**, if required.
9. **Payment of the Commercial Court registry fees** via mobile banking (500 MRU for corporate entities).

## Key things to know

- The **Articles of Association** (statuts) are the founding document: they define the company name, purpose, capital, contributions, and governance bodies. They must be drafted (often with the help of a notary or lawyer) **before** going to the One-Stop Shop.
- If the **share capital exceeds 10,000 MRU**, the release of capital must be evidenced (bank deposit or manager's declaration).
- The **registered office** must be in Mauritania; a physical premises is mandatory.
- APIM **does not provide legal advice**: for the choice between SARL and SA, or for drafting the articles, consult an independent law firm.

## Legal basis

Same as for the sole proprietorship:

- Decree no. 2022-169 of 17 November 2022 (unified form).
- Order no. 1487/MJ/2021 of 13 December 2021 (commercial registry, beneficial owner).
- Decree no. 2021-033 of 11 March 2021 (Commercial Registry and Movable Securities Register).
- Law no. 2021-005 of 10 February 2021 (Commercial Code).
- Law no. 2019-018 of 29 April 2019 (General Tax Code).
- Law no. 2021-007 of 22 February 2021 (Social Security).
- Order no. 1974-116 of 17 September 1974 (CNSS).

## Turnaround and cost

- **Turnaround**: 48 working hours after a complete file (including articles) is submitted.
- **APIM service fees**: free.
- **Registry fees**: 500 MRU (mobile banking).
- **Additional costs not covered**: notary/lawyer fees for the articles, capital deposit, etc.
