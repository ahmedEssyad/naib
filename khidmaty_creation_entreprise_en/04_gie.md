## Definition

An **Economic Interest Group (Groupement d'Intérêt Économique, GIE)** is formed between **two or more natural or legal persons**, for a **determined duration**, to deploy means designed to **facilitate or develop the economic activity of its members** and to improve or increase the results of that activity.

Characteristics:

- The group's activity must be **essentially linked to its members' economic activity**; it can only have an **auxiliary character**.
- A GIE **does not by itself give rise to profit-making and distribution** — it is not a stand-alone for-profit company.
- The group may be set up **with or without capital**.
- It has its **own separate legal personality**.

Typical uses: pooled purchasing, sharing technical resources, cooperation between SMEs to bid on larger contracts.

## Full file required

1. **Unified Business Creation Form**, duly filled in and signed.
2. **A certified copy of the group agreement**.
3. **Photocopies of the Commercial Registry extracts of the group's members**.
4. **Photocopies of the Articles of Association of the members** (for members that are themselves legal entities).
5. **A certificate of deposit of the group agreement at the Commercial Court registry**.
6. **A copy of the certified list of managers, directors, officers or partners** who are indefinitely and personally liable, or who have the power to bind the group.
7. **Four (4) photocopies of the ID of the manager or director**.
8. **Two (2) photocopies of the ID of each declared employee**.
9. **Two (2) certified copies of the property title, lease, or occupancy certificate** for the group's registered office + **electricity or water bill**.
10. **Two (2) photocopies of the bank account opening certificate in the group's name**.
11. **Payment of the Commercial Court registry fees** via mobile banking.

## Key things to know

- The **group agreement** (the equivalent of articles of association for a GIE) must define the purpose, duration, members, governance arrangements, and identity of the managers.
- **Each member remains jointly and severally liable** for the group's debts (unlike in an SARL).
- If all members are **legal entities already registered**, their Commercial Registry extracts and articles must be attached.
