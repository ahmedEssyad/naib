# Opening a Branch in Mauritania (Succursale)

SOURCE: PDF "Dossier de Création — Une Succursale", APIM, October 2025
URL_PDF: https://apim.gov.mr/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Dossier-de-Creation-Fr-1-2.pdf
ORGANISME: APIM — One-Stop Shop for Business Registration
DATE_INDEXATION: 2026-05-17

## Definition

A **branch (succursale)** is a secondary establishment **without its own legal personality**, with a degree of **management autonomy**, that a company called the « parent company » decides to set up in a particular geographic area — in this case, Mauritania.

Key consequence: **the branch's rights and obligations remain attached to the parent company**. The branch is not an autonomous Mauritanian business; it's a local extension of the parent.

This form is favoured by **foreign companies** that want to operate in Mauritania without creating a separate subsidiary.

## Two variants: by nature of the parent company

The file differs depending on whether the **parent company** is a natural or a legal person.

### Variant A — Parent = Natural person

1. **Unified Business Creation Form**, duly filled in and signed.
2. **An up-to-date Commercial Registry extract of the parent company**.
3. **Four (4) photocopies of the branch director's ID**, or residence card (resident foreigners), or passport (non-resident foreigners).
4. **Two (2) photocopies of the ID of each declared employee**.
5. **A photocopy of the administrative authorisation to carry out the declared activity**, if required.
6. **Two (2) certified copies of the property title, lease or occupancy certificate** for the branch's main establishment + **electricity or water bill**.
7. **Two (2) photocopies of the bank account opening certificate in the branch's name**.
8. **Payment of the Commercial Court registry fees** via mobile banking.

### Variant B — Parent = Legal entity

Same documents as Variant A, **plus**:

- **One certified copy and three (3) photocopies of the parent company's Articles of Association**.
- **A certified copy of the resolution creating the branch and appointing its director** (board resolution or equivalent corporate act).

## Key things to know

- **No separate legal personality**: the branch cannot, for example, enter into contracts in its own name independently of the parent; the parent remains legally bound.
- **No mandatory own capital**.
- The **branch director** is mandatory and must be appointed by a parent-company resolution.
- If the parent is foreign, its documents (articles, Commercial Registry extract) must be presented; they often need to be **translated and legalised**.

## Legal basis

Same as for the other forms (see also any international conventions applicable in the parent company's country of origin):

- Decree no. 2022-169 of 17 November 2022 (unified form).
- Order no. 1487/MJ/2021 of 13 December 2021.
- Decree no. 2021-033 of 11 March 2021.
- Law no. 2021-005 of 10 February 2021 (Commercial Code).
- Law no. 2019-018 of 29 April 2019 (General Tax Code).
- Law no. 2021-007 of 22 February 2021 (Social Security).
- Order no. 1974-116 of 17 September 1974 (CNSS).

## Turnaround and cost

- **Turnaround**: 48 working hours after a complete file is submitted.
- **APIM service fees**: free.
- **Registry fees**: payable via mobile banking.
