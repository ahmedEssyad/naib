# FAQ — Setting Up a Business in Mauritania

SOURCE: https://apim.gov.mr/investor-services/business-setup/
ORGANISME: APIM — One-Stop Shop for Business Registration
DATE_INDEXATION: 2026-05-17

Frequently asked questions, written to answer a citizen / entrepreneur calling or talking to the Khidmaty voice assistant.

---

**Q1. How long does it take to set up my business?**
48 working hours after a complete file is submitted at the APIM One-Stop Shop. If a document is missing, the clock only starts once the file is regularised.

---

**Q2. Can I set up my business online, without travelling?**
Yes, via the **Khidmaty platform** (https://khidmaty.gov.mr — APIM's official online registration portal). Otherwise, in person at the One-Stop Shop, Avenue Mokhtar Ould Daddah, Tevragh Zeina, Nouakchott.

---

**Q3. What types of companies can I create?**
Four forms:
- **Sole proprietorship** (personne physique) — for a single entrepreneur, no minimum capital.
- **Limited liability company (SARL)** — the most common corporate form.
- **Public limited company (SA)** — for projects needing larger capital.
- **Economic Interest Group (GIE)** — for several entities pooling resources.
- **Branch** — for a foreign company establishing itself.

---

**Q4. Is it mandatory to have a physical office in Mauritania?**
**Yes.** A local address is required for registration. You must provide a property title, lease, or occupancy certificate, plus a water or electricity bill in the premises' name.

---

**Q5. Does APIM provide legal assistance for drafting the articles of association?**
**No.** APIM facilitates the administrative procedure but does not provide legal advice. For drafting articles (SARL, SA, GIE) or choosing between legal forms, consult an **independent law firm or notary**.

---

**Q6. I am a foreigner. Can I set up a business in Mauritania?**
**Yes.** Foreign investors enjoy the **same rights as nationals**:
- Freedom to invest.
- Protection against arbitrary expropriation.
- Right to repatriate capital and profits, subject to exchange controls.

Additional documents required from a foreigner:
- Passport (instead of national ID) or residence card.
- Marriage certificate (for a sole proprietorship).

---

**Q7. Can I repatriate my profits abroad?**
**Yes**, after payment of applicable taxes, subject to banking and exchange-control regulations in Mauritania.

---

**Q8. How much does it cost exactly?**
- **APIM service: free**.
- **Commercial Court registry fees** (paid via mobile banking):
  - **200 MRU** for a sole proprietorship (personne physique).
  - **500 MRU** for a corporate entity (personne morale).
- Additional costs: any notary/lawyer fees for the articles, capital deposit, bank account fees.

---

**Q9. Is a minimum capital required?**
- **Sole proprietorship**: **no** minimum capital.
- **Corporate entity (personne morale)**: variable by form. **If the share capital exceeds 10,000 MRU**, a deposit certificate or manager's declaration is required.
- **GIE**: with or without capital, as you choose.
- **Branch**: no own capital — attached to the parent company.

---

**Q10. What registrations does the One-Stop Shop handle for me?**
Three registrations in a single step:
1. **Commercial Registry** (Commercial Court).
2. **Tax registration** (DGI — NIF number).
3. **Social Security registration** (CNSS).
You avoid making the round of three administrations.

---

**Q11. My activity is regulated (transport, health, security…). What should I do?**
You must obtain a **prior administrative authorisation** from the ministry overseeing your sector **before** submitting your file to the One-Stop Shop. Without this authorisation, the file cannot be processed.

---

**Q12. My business will hire employees. Should I include them at creation?**
If you're hiring **from creation**, yes: provide **two ID photocopies of each employee** in the initial file. CNSS registration will be done simultaneously. If you hire later, employee registration will be done later.

---

**Q13. How do I pay the registry fees? Can I pay in cash at the counter?**
**No.** Payment is made **only via mobile banking**, on the accounts opened for this purpose by the One-Stop Shop. Ask for the payment procedure at the One-Stop Shop or on the Khidmaty platform.

---

**Q14. After registration — what should I do next?**
Once the business is registered:
- You receive your **Commercial Registry extract**, your **NIF** (tax identification number), and your **CNSS registration**.
- You can begin operating.
- You must comply with your tax obligations (periodic declarations) and social obligations (CNSS contributions if you have employees).
- Any change (registered office, manager, capital) must be declared to the Commercial Court.
